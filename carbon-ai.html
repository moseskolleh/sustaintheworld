Methodology & sources

Per-query carbon = (Wh per 1k tokens × tokens / 1000 / 1000) × PUE × grid intensity. Numbers are order-of-magnitude estimates, not measurements; real workloads vary by hardware (H100 vs. TPU v5), batching, KV-cache reuse, and quantisation.

  • Per-token energy bands: Patterson et al. 2021 ("Carbon Emissions and Large Neural Network Training"); Luccioni, Viguier & Ligozat 2023 ("Power Hungry Processing"); de Vries 2023 ("The growing energy footprint of AI", Joule).
  • Frontier-model query energy anchored on Epoch AI & SemiAnalysis 2024 estimates (~0.3 Wh / short ChatGPT request).
  • Grid intensity (gCO₂e/kWh): Ember Climate Yearly Electricity Data 2023, IEA Electricity 2024.
  • Equivalents: EEA 2022 (gasoline car ~170 gCO₂e/km), Berners-Lee How Bad Are Bananas? (~8 g per phone charge), US EPA & US Forest Service (~21 kg CO₂e absorbed per mature tree per year).
  • PUE defaults to 1.2 — typical for hyperscalers (Google 2023: 1.10; Meta: 1.08; industry avg ~1.5).
Caveat: figures here cover inference only. Training is amortised across millions of queries and is not modelled. Embodied carbon of GPUs is also excluded. Use this as a relative comparator, not an absolute audit.
