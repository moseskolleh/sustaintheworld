Coastal Water Pollution Dynamics

What drives rivers to pollute the world's coasts? A global analysis of socioeconomic drivers — and future storylines for Africa's coastal waters.

PythonRQGISScenario Building

The challenge

Rivers carry whatever we put in them straight to the sea. I set out to untangle how income, urbanisation and agriculture shape the export of multiple pollutants across the world's sub-basins — and what that means for Africa's coasts by mid-century.

My approach

  • Analysed global river-export data for multiple pollutants across sub-basins
  • Identified the socioeconomic drivers behind pollution patterns statistically
  • Built qualitative future storylines for African coastal waters — including the pessimistic "Dirty Delta" scenario
  • Integrated climate-change scenarios into the projections

Results & impact

  • Pinned down the critical socioeconomic factors behind coastal pollution
  • Delivered scenario storylines that turn model output into policy conversations
  • Contributed to global understanding of water pollution patterns

2016 — 2017 · Freetown, SL BSc Dissertation

Groundwater Potential Mapping

Geophysics that became drinking water: mapping aquifers in the Freetown Complex, then proving it in the field with a 70% strike rate.

GeophysicsGISHydrogeology

The challenge

The Freetown Complex is geologically stubborn — hard igneous rock where finding water is a gamble. Communities were paying for dry boreholes. The science needed to de-risk the drill bit.

My approach

  • Applied geophysical surveying (resistivity methods) to detect groundwater
  • Combined geological and hydrogeological data into GIS potential maps
  • Validated predictions through field drilling campaigns
  • Produced siting recommendations for future boreholes

Results & impact

  • 70% aquifer strike rate in subsequent drilling — well above local averages
  • Groundwater potential maps now underpinning local water development
  • This research later guided the 164 water points I helped deliver
